About

Born in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria in 1956, Xano Armenter has spent five decades navigating the space between what we see and what we perceive.

His inner drive to recreate visual impressions began with the onset of an eye illness at the age of two, when he also lost his mother. Not having his sight for extended periods transformed the way he saw and appreciated everything around him. When his eyesight returned, his perception of the world became vibrant and alive in ways most people never experience.

After studying art and design at the Escola Eina in Barcelona under experimental teachers, he moved to New York in 1980. During the vibrant East Village days of the 1980s, he worked with Milton Glaser, who became his mentor. The energy of New York's graffiti landscapes and downtown art scene left a permanent mark on his practice.

He moved to Los Angeles in 1993, where his language shifted toward an abstraction that never fully detaches from its figurative roots. His work has blossomed across mediums and contexts — from canvas to fresco walls, from rugs to wine bottles, from high-end hotels to hospital corridors — in America, Europe, and Asia.

To him, painting is a joyous visual chess problem — one that challenges both artist and observer to question and decipher the nature of art as a reflection of the external world.

Currently teaching illustration, color, and graphic design at Istituto Europeo di Design (IED). President of Barcelona's historic Cercle Artístic de Sant Lluc. Represented by KBH Fine Art.
